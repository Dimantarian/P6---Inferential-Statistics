Background¶

This project will evaluate whether or not the Stroop task highlights a statistically significant difference in the way in which the human brain processes visuals. Data has been collected from a series of trials where the participant’s task is to say out loud the color of the ink in which the word is printed.

The task has two conditions:

  • A congruent words condition, and an incongruent words condition. In the congruent words condition, the words being displayed are color words whose names match the colors in which they are printed: for example **RED**, **BLUE**.
  • In the incongruent words condition, the words displayed are color words whose names do not match the colors in which they are printed: for example **PURPLE**, **ORANGE**.

In each case, we measure the time it takes to name the ink colors in equally-sized lists. Each participant will go through and record a time from each condition.

Questions for Investigation¶

In this section we will set up the statistical analysis of the experiment, detailing the design choices and reasoning.

Variables¶

Our dependent Variable of for analysis is the time taken to complete the Stroop test. Our independent variable or our treatment is whether or not the word, colour pairings are congruent, as described in the background section.

Hypothesis test structure¶

The data provided for this test contains two samples, taken across one set of individuals.Given it is a sample of individuals pre/post treatment, and we do not have the descriptive statistics for the whole population (7+bn!), so a dependant t-Test is most appropriate. We will be testing the following hypothesis:

**H0:** $\mu_0 - \mu_A = 0$
**HA:** $\mu_0 - \mu_A \neq 0$

In plain english, our null hypothesis, HA is that there is no change in mean time to read the words between pre/post treatment. Our alternative hypothesis, HA is that there is a change.

This requires a two-tailed test. For conventions sake we will choose $\alpha = 0.05$.

Conducting the test¶

First up, what are our parameters?

Degrees of Freedom: 23 Alpha $\alpha$: 0.05 Test Type: Two tailed tTest

By looking at the tTest table from the lectures, it appears that our critical T is +- 2.069.

## Conduct t-Test
raw_data.tTest = sp.stats.ttest_rel(raw_data.iloc[:,0], raw_data.iloc[:,1])
t = raw_data.tTest[0]
p = raw_data.tTest[1]

print "The t-statistic is %.3f and the p-value is %.3f." % raw_data.tTest

The t-statistic is -8.021 and the p-value is 0.000.

Given our p-value is so low and of course, our t-statistic far below our critical t, the difference in means is statistically significant at an $\alpha$ of 0.05.
